## 1. Executive Summary

The Human Resources Department proposes the implementation of a “Quarterly Wall of Fame” Employee Recognition Program. This initiative is designed to formalize and celebrate the outstanding contributions of our workforce across various operational and cultural dimensions. 

The primary objective is to cultivate a culture of appreciation, motivating employees by publicly acknowledging exceptional performance, strict adherence to safety protocols, innovative thinking, and positive workplace behavior. By implementing a structured, transparent, and multi-category recognition system, the organization expects to see immediate improvements in employee morale, a reduction in absenteeism, enhanced productivity, and a stronger alignment with our core corporate values.

---

## 2. Background and Need for the Program

In today’s competitive environment, employee engagement is a critical driver of organizational success. Recent internal feedback and industry benchmarks indicate that employees who feel valued and recognized are significantly more likely to be highly engaged, productive, and committed to their employer.

Currently, while exceptional work occurs daily, our organization lacks a formalized, highly visible platform to celebrate these achievements consistently. This absence can lead to decreased motivation, a sense of under-appreciation, and higher turnover rates among top performers. There is a distinct need for a transparent, objective, and motivating reward system that goes beyond standard compensation to fulfill the psychological and emotional needs of our workforce, thereby boosting overall productivity and morale.

---

## 3. Objectives of the Wall of Fame Program

The implementation of this program aims to achieve the following strategic goals:

* **Encourage Regular Attendance:** Reduce absenteeism and tardiness.
* **Promote Workplace Safety:** Reinforce a zero-harm culture and adherence to safety protocols.
* **Motivate Innovative Suggestions:** Stimulate continuous improvement and cost-saving ideas.
* **Improve Cleanliness and Discipline:** Foster a well-maintained, orderly, and professional work environment.
* **Encourage Teamwork and Productivity:** Highlight collaborative successes and high-output performance.
* **Enhance Company Culture:** Build a supportive and positive internal community.
* **Increase Employee Retention and Morale:** Retain top talent by making them feel visible and valued.

## 4. Award Categories (Detailed)

To ensure inclusive recognition across all departments and roles, the program will feature the following distinct award categories, evaluated quarterly:

### 4.1. Best Attendance Award
* **Eligibility:** All permanent and contract employees.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** 100% attendance, zero unapproved late arrivals, no early departures.
* **KPI Examples:** Time and Attendance system records.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Cash Reward, and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Quality Team Representative
* **Evaluator(s):** HR Manager & Department Heads

### 4.2. Safety Champion Award
* **Eligibility:** All employees, particularly those in operational or manufacturing roles.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Proactive identification of hazards, zero safety incidents/near-misses, active participation in safety drills, and championing safe practices among peers.
* **KPI Examples:** Number of hazard reports submitted, safety audit scores.
* **Award Type:** Trophy, Certificate, Gift Voucher, and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Safety Officer & Production/Operations Manager

### 4.3. Best Suggestion / Innovation Award
* **Eligibility:** All employees.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Submission of actionable ideas that improve processes, reduce costs, enhance quality, or increase efficiency.
* **KPI Examples:** Estimated cost savings, time saved, process improvement metrics.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Cash Reward (percentage of savings or fixed tier), and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Production/Operations Manager & Department Heads

### 4.4. Cleanliness & Workplace Discipline Award (5S Champion)
* **Eligibility:** Teams or individuals.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Maintaining a pristine work area, adherence to 5S principles, and strict compliance with company policies and dress codes.
* **KPI Examples:** 5S audit scores, absence of disciplinary warnings.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Gift Voucher, and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Quality Team Representative & Department Heads
* **Evaluator(s):** Safety Officer & HR Manager

### 4.5. Best Team Player Award
* **Eligibility:** All employees.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Willingness to assist colleagues, fostering collaboration, conflict resolution, and contributing to a positive team dynamic.
* **KPI Examples:** Peer nominations, 360-degree feedback scores.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Appreciation Letter, Lunch with Leadership.
* **Evaluator(s):** Employee Representative & HR Manager

### 4.6. Productivity Excellence Award
* **Eligibility:** Production, Operations, and Sales teams.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Consistently exceeding production targets, sales quotas, or operational output metrics without compromising quality.
* **KPI Examples:** Units produced, sales targets achieved, efficiency percentages.
* **Award Type:** Trophy, Cash Reward, and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Production/Operations Manager

### 4.7. Customer Service Excellence Award
* **Eligibility:** Client-facing and support staff.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Exceptional internal or external customer feedback, resolving complex issues, and maintaining a high standard of service.
* **KPI Examples:** CSAT scores, positive client testimonials, resolution times.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Gift Voucher, and Wall of Fame feature.

### 4.8. Leadership Initiative Award
* **Eligibility:** Non-managerial staff who step up, or junior managers.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Taking charge during crises, mentoring peers, and driving projects to completion without explicit direction.
* **KPI Examples:** Project success rates, mentorship feedback.
* **Award Type:** Plaque, Reserved Parking (1 Month), and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** Department Heads & HR Manager

### 4.9. Quality Excellence Award
* **Eligibility:** All operational and administrative staff.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Delivering work with zero defects, strict adherence to quality standards, and minimizing rework.
* **KPI Examples:** Defect rates, QA audit results, accuracy metrics.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Cash Reward, and Wall of Fame feature.

### 4.10. Rising Star / Emerging Talent Award
* **Eligibility:** Employees with less than 1 year of tenure.
* **Evaluation Criteria:** Rapid onboarding, immediate positive impact, high learning agility, and exceptional early performance.
* **KPI Examples:** Probation review scores, milestone achievements.
* **Award Type:** Certificate, Memento, and Wall of Fame feature.
* **Evaluator(s):** HR Manager & Department Heads

## 5. Selection Committee Structure

To ensure fairness, transparency, and cross-departmental representation, a formal Selection Committee will be established.

### Committee Composition
* **HR Manager:** Committee Chairperson (facilitates the process).
* **Department Heads:** Functional experts to validate nominations.
* **Safety Officer:** Assesses safety-related nominations.
* **Production/Operations Manager:** Assesses productivity and quality metrics.
* **Quality Team Representative:** Validates quality excellence claims.
* **Employee Representative:** Elected/Rotated staff member to ensure ground-level perspective.

### Roles and Guidelines
* **Voting Process:** Anonymous scoring based on a predefined matrix. A minimum of 70% consensus is required for an award.
* **Transparency Guidelines:** All scoring criteria and final decisions will be documented and available for high-level audit.
* **Conflict of Interest Policy:** Committee members cannot vote for their direct relatives or if they have a direct personal interest. They must recuse themselves from such votes.

---

## 6. Nomination and Evaluation Process

The process will be conducted quarterly following this workflow:

1. **Week 1: Call for Nominations:** HR announces the opening of nominations via Intranet and notice boards. Employees can self-nominate or nominate peers. Managers submit their nominations.
2. **Week 2: Department Review:** Department Heads filter and validate nominations within their units based on KPIs.
3. **Week 3: HR Verification:** HR conducts background checks (e.g., verifying attendance, ensuring no active disciplinary actions).
4. **Week 4: Committee Evaluation:** The Selection Committee meets to review verified nominations using the evaluation scorecard and finalize the winners.
5. **Week 5: Management Approval:** Final list is sent to the Executive Team for formal sign-off.
6. **Week 6: Announcement & Award Ceremony:** Winners are announced in a Town Hall meeting, and the Wall of Fame is updated.

## 7. Reward and Recognition Structure

To provide meaningful recognition, the reward structure includes a mix of monetary, physical, and experiential benefits.

### Reward Items
* **Certificates of Excellence:** Framed and signed by the CEO and HR Head.
* **Trophy/Memento:** Custom engraved acrylic or glass awards.
* **Gift Vouchers:** E-vouchers for popular retail or grocery outlets.
* **Cash Rewards:** Processed through payroll (subject to tax).
* **Appreciation Letters:** Placed in the employee's permanent HR file.
* **Wall of Fame Feature:** Prominent display on the physical wall and Intranet homepage.
* **Experiential Rewards:** Reserved premium parking spot for the quarter, or an exclusive Lunch with Leadership.

## 8. Wall of Fame Display Plan

The program requires a high-visibility platform to maximize its impact.

### Physical Wall Setup
* **Location:** Main reception area, cafeteria, or central employee walkway.
* **Design:** A branded, professionally designed installation featuring the company logo, program title, and high-quality acrylic frames.
* **Content:** Professional headshots of the winners, their names, departments, and a brief 2-sentence description of their achievement.

### Digital Intranet Section
* **Location:** A dedicated banner and sub-page on the internal company Intranet portal.
* **Content:** Digital badges, full achievement stories, interview snippets, and a historical archive of past winners.

## 9. Policy Guidelines

* **Eligibility Rules:** Nominees must be active employees, off probation (except for Rising Star), with a minimum of 6 months of continuous service.
* **Disqualification Conditions:** Any employee with an active disciplinary warning, formal performance improvement plan (PIP), or unexcused absences during the quarter is ineligible.
* **Ethical Behavior Expectations:** Nominations must be based on factual data. Falsifying records or collusion to secure votes will result in disciplinary action.
* **Review and Appeal Process:** Decisions of the Selection Committee are final. Feedback will be provided to managers of unsuccessful nominees to encourage future development.

## 10. Benefits to the Organization

Implementing the Wall of Fame will yield significant organizational dividends:
* **Improved Morale:** Fosters a positive, appreciative work environment.
* **Reduced Absenteeism:** Direct financial incentive for consistent attendance.
* **Better Safety Compliance:** Reduces workplace accidents and associated costs.
* **Increased Innovation:** Harnesses the collective intelligence of the workforce.
* **Stronger Teamwork:** Breaks down silos and encourages cross-functional support.
* **Higher Productivity:** Healthy competition drives operational output.
* **Positive Workplace Culture:** Enhances employer brand, aiding in talent attraction and retention.

## 11. Risk and Mitigation Plan

| Potential Challenge | Mitigation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bias or Favoritism in Selection** | Use a diverse committee, strict KPI-driven scorecards, and anonymous voting. |
| **Employee Dissatisfaction (Losers)** | Ensure transparent criteria; encourage managers to provide constructive feedback and recognize effort locally. |
| **Budget Constraints** | Implement tiered rewards; focus on high-visibility, low-cost recognition (Intranet, certificates, parking spots). |
| **Lack of Participation** | Drive aggressive internal marketing campaigns; require managers to submit at least one nomination per quarter. |
